Motion Retargetting

Trained on Style GAN

    This was my final year project at CUHK, and was accomplished with my teammate Vaan, Huang.
During the first phase, we tried non linear optimization function provided by Matlab to copy the motion from one skeleton to another. We found out that by fixing the end points of skeleton to the position of the original motion will hit and look for optimal solution (minimal distance between the retargetted position and the original position of joints) will cause some un-natural glitch that the original motion doesn't have. (For example an optimal solution for knees may be behind the torso)
    By knowing the limitation of optimizing functions, we decided to try on GAN networks. We first do a feasibility test to see if GAN network are capable of producing skeletons. We trained with about 10,000 frames of different hand-leg ratio skeletons, and the results were quite promising. With a promising result from feasibility test, we decided to do motion retargetting with GAN network. We adopted Style GAN developed by NVIDIA, and tried to generate a series of frames that can ultimately be played as a smooth motion. This network first train in a lower resolution manner, and then gradually upgrade to 1024x1024. We planned to train with 10,000 kimg, but we were only able to train to around 7,000 kimg until the end of the semester. With an uncomplete training, the result was quite unsatisfying; and the grading professor regard our method as "not actually" achieving motion retargetting, because we didn't actually retarget the skeleton but reducing the problem from 3D to 2D producing a seemingly satisfying result.
    The words of the professor was frustrating but also very useful. There are no easy way on tackling a real world problem; and if a problem is beyond one's capability, he should sharpen his skills. I reached out to seniors on this topic, and gathered some idea on doing motion retargetting that could be a solution on further development, such as deep learning with point clouds.

Report Generator

Developed independently while working at Cellmax Life

    Cellmax used to dedicate a person to generate the report by copying data from test results at the lab to templates. The process was tedious and uneffcient; moreover, human errors happened sometimes harming the credibility of the report. The work was not difficult at all, but it was time-consuming and could easily err.
    I decided to make a desktop application which could automate the process. The report required a line graph that shows the tendency of patients Circulating Tumor Cell count, while other fields required look up from the Google Spreadsheets that they store them. I coded in a multi-thread manner which one thread draws the line graph while the other fills in the blank. The whole program was based on python, including the user interface.
    The program reduced the time of producing a report from more than 30 minutes to less than a minute without typo and other errors. However, I found out the storage structure was very messy, and they store all the data on Google Spreadsheets; it was not a smart way to store client data at a company level. Thus, I started to develop a database system to store the data systematically, which can be found in the next card in portfolio section.

MySQL Database

Google Cloud MySQL database

    Discovering the floppy structure of Cellmax data storage, I start to design a databse to store the data in an organized way.
    I reached out to the lab, who stores the results of tests, and administation office, who actually put in the client information for understanding the structure of the information. Also, since the lab of Cellmax was certified by CAP , which there was regulations about the data storage that people in the lab should not have access to the patient information except for a serial code, I have to build a database that follows the regulation.
With the knowing of rules and the structure of data, I designed a MySQL database that was held by Google Cloud Service and normalized the tables to Boyce-Codd normal form.
    After building the database and transferring the data from spreadsheets to database, I started to build a front-end web application to view, add, and edit data to the database. I built it with Vue.js for updating information from the database in real time, and php for updating the information to tables.
    I was preparing for master application at that period, and I decided to dedicate to my application; after discussion with the vice president, I resigned from Cellmax, and the VP invited me to be their IT consultant once my application comes to an end. So the development was suspended at this moment, and I will keep on perfecting the database if I become the IT consultant at Cellmax someday.
